# Golden Handcuffs — Retention Manuals for HR Professionals

A short-form manual on golden handcuffs (equity vesting, deferred compensation, non-compete clauses, and related retention instruments) — written for Heads of HR, Comp&Ben leads, and non-HR founders who need to make compensation decisions without walking blindly into loss-aversion economics.

Available in **English (UK)** and **Romanian**, with cross-jurisdictional notes on UK case law (*Tillman v Egon Zehnder* [2019] UKSC 32) and the Romanian Labour Code (Articles 21–24).

## What the articles cover

- **The mechanics of loss aversion** — why handcuffs work through prospect theory (Kahneman & Tversky, 1979), not through positive incentive
- **A taxonomy of instruments** — equity/RSU vesting, sign-on bonuses with clawback, LTIPs, deferred compensation, EMI options, phantom shares, non-compete clauses
- **Four theoretical lenses** — Job Embeddedness (Mitchell et al., 2001), Two-Factor Theory (Herzberg, 1959), Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan, 1985), Psychological Contract (Rousseau, 1989)
- **A decision matrix** — performance × role criticality, with explicit guidance on which quadrant justifies handcuffs
- **A case study** — Netflix's "no handcuffs" philosophy (Patty McCord's Keeper Test) versus Meta's RSU-based model and its 2022 market stress test
- **Three traps** — retention theatre, legal risk (including correct reading of *Tillman*), long-term motivation crowding-out
- **Jurisdictional notes** — UK restrictive covenant practice (6–12 months typical) and Romanian Labour Code mechanics (non-compete, max 2 years, minimum 50% consideration)

---

## Intended audience

- **Heads of HR / Comp&Ben leads** working on retention strategy at a structural level
- **Founders and C-suite executives** (non-HR) who need to understand what their HR team is proposing
- **HR consultants** advising clients on compensation architecture
- **Students and academics** in organisational behaviour looking for a practitioner-oriented synthesis

Not written for: HR generalists looking for quick-win tactics, or for roles where the compensation decision-making sits elsewhere.
